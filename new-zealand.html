About

New Zealand is an independant island-nation that is located in the Pacific Ocean, just about 1,300 miles south east of Australia.

It is believed that Polynesian Settlers may have arrived to New Zealand in the late 1200's

Once a colony of the UK, it declared its independence in 1947.

Geography

New Zealand is located in Oceania, in the islands in the South Pacific Ocean, southeast of Australia.

Area

  • Total: 268,838 sq km
  • Land: 264,537 sq km
  • Water: 4,301 sq km

Area, Comparative

New Zealand is almost twice the size of the US state, North Carolina and is roughly the same size as US state Colorado.

Land Use

New Zealand's land use is primarily for agricultural purposes, which makes up 43.2% of the total land area. The breakdown is as follows:

  1. Permanent Pasture: 41.1% (2018 est.)
  2. Arable Land: 1.8% (2018 est.)
  3. Permanent Crops: 0.3% (2018 est.)

Forests also make up 31.4% of the land

Environment

Given New Zealand's geographical location and land mass composure, the environment is a steadfast subject.

Current Issues

Current issues that New Zealand is facing includes, but is not limited to:

  • Water quality and availability
  • Rapid urbanisation
  • Negative effects of climate change
  • Deforestation
  • Soil erosion and degradation
  • Native flora and fauna hard-hit by invasive species

Urbanization

Currently, New Zealand consists of 86.9% of its residents in urban locations.

New Zealand's rate of urbanization is currently at a 0.92% rate of change.

Communications

Despite being a small island nation, New Zealand has a robust communications infrastructure

Telecommunications

New Zealand has an excellent domestic and international systems with process in mobile services, with their LTE network rates among some of the fastest in the world.

Their top tier investment and development of infrastructure enabled network capabilities to propel the digital economy, e-government, and e-commerce across the country.

However, COVID-19 has hindered progress in supply chains globally. This has impacted New Zealands ability to widely implement 5G networks. Though, the need for telecommunications technology during the pandemic provided some government issued aide.

Broadcast Media

State-owned Television New Zealand operates multiple TV networks, and state-owned Radio New Zealand operates 3 radio networks and an external shortwave radio service to the South Pacific region.

A small number of national commercial TV and radio stations and many regional commercial television and radio stations are available; cable and satellite TV systems are available, as are a range of streaming services

Internet

  • Internet Country Code is .nz
  • Total population of internet users is 4.55 million (2021 est.)
  • Total percent of population connected to the internet is 90.81% (2019 est.)

While 90.81% of their population is connected, New Zealand only ranks 98th in the world trailing behind Paraguy, Bulgaria, and Slovakia

However, they do move up in the rankings with broadband subscriptions. A total of 1.647 million citizens have access to broadband, which is about 33.67%. This places New Zealand at number 63 in the world, trailing behind Finland, Serbia, and Slovakia.
